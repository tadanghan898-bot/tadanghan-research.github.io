What We Do

Research Areas

Our lab focuses on making AI more accessible, efficient, and powerful through research at the intersection of systems, algorithms, and infrastructure.

⚙

Efficient Neural Architecture Search

Developing novel approaches to automatically discover optimal neural network architectures with minimal computational cost. Targeting 60-80% reduction in search cost compared to traditional NAS methods.

💻

Distributed GPU Training Systems

Building next-generation distributed training frameworks that optimize multi-GPU synchronization, gradient compression, and memory management for large-scale neural network training.

🚀

Parameter-Efficient LLM Fine-tuning

Research on LoRA, QLoRA, and adapter-based methods enabling fine-tuning of billion-parameter models on consumer-grade GPUs. Making large language models accessible to everyone.

⚡

AI Inference Optimization

Model quantization, pruning, knowledge distillation, and efficient serving architectures. Reducing inference latency and cost by 10x while maintaining model quality.

🎮

Cloud GPU Infrastructure

Optimizing GPU cloud resource allocation, cost-efficiency, and scheduling algorithms for machine learning workloads across distributed computing environments.

🧠

AI for Science

Applying deep learning and neural network techniques to scientific computing problems including computational biology, physics simulations, and materials science.

EfficientNAS: Gradient-Free Neural Architecture Search via Stochastic Perturbation for Resource-Constrained Environments

Tadang Han

Preprint, arXiv:2026.XXXXX (2026)

We present EfficientNAS, a novel gradient-free neural architecture search method that reduces computational cost by 70% compared to traditional approaches. Our method uses stochastic perturbation-based evaluation combined with transfer learning from pre-trained backbones, enabling architecture search on a single GPU within 24 hours.

DistributedGrad: Optimizing Multi-GPU Synchronization for Large-Scale Neural Network Training

Tadang Han

Under Review at ICLR 2027 (2026)

We propose DistributedGrad, a novel communication-efficient distributed training framework that reduces inter-GPU synchronization overhead by 65% through gradient compression and asynchronous parameter updates. Benchmarks on 64-GPU clusters show 2.3x speedup over standard data parallelism.

Distributed Training GPU Computing Gradient Compression
📜 Paper 💻 Code

LoRA-X: Adaptive Low-Rank Adaptation for Domain-Specific LLM Fine-tuning

Tadang Han

Under Review at NeurIPS 2026 (2026)

We introduce LoRA-X, an adaptive parameter-efficient fine-tuning method that dynamically adjusts rank allocation based on layer importance scores. On downstream tasks, LoRA-X achieves 15% improvement over standard LoRA with the same parameter budget, enabling high-quality fine-tuning on consumer-grade GPUs.
