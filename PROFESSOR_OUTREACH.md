# PART 3: SENDING STRATEGY

## Step 1: Personalize Each Email
- Reference a SPECIFIC paper (not just "your work")
- Explain WHY you care about their specific contribution
- Connect it to YOUR research clearly

## Step 2: Timing
- Send Tuesday-Thursday mornings (Vietnam time: 8-10 AM)
- Best months: January-February (PhD apps), September-October (postdoc)
- Avoid: Right before major deadlines, holidays

## Step 3: Follow Up
- If no response in 2 weeks, send 1 polite follow-up
- Maximum 2 follow-ups total
- Be gracious regardless of response

## Step 4: Track
- Use a spreadsheet to track: Professor, University, Email, Sent Date, Response, Status
- Note what you referenced in each email

# PART 4: GITHUB ACADEMIC ADVICE

## Building Credibility Fast:
1. **Open source contributions** - Contribute to popular ML repos (transformers, pytorch, etc.)
2. **Blog posts** - Write about your research on Medium, personal blog
3. **arXiv preprints** - Post your work immediately
4. **Conference attendance** - NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML have workshops + mentoring programs
5. **Workshop papers** - Easier to get in than main track

## Free Conference Opportunities:
- **NeurIPS Datasets and Benchmarks Track** - Industry datasets welcome
- **ICLR Workshop** - Easier acceptance, good networking
- **NeurIPS workshop** - Many workshops accept non-peer-reviewed work
- **ICML Workshop** - Similar to ICLR

# PART 5: REALISTIC PATHWAY

## Path A: University Affiliation
1. Email professor → get response → visit as research intern
2. After 3-6 months collaboration → formal visiting researcher status
3. After co-authored paper → ask for PhD/postdoc recommendation

## Path B: Research Lab Affiliation
1. Apply to industry research labs (Google Brain, Meta AI, NVIDIA Research)
2. These have research scientist roles for non-PhDs
3. Build portfolio with papers + GitHub contributions

## Path C: Independent → Legitimize
1. Publish 2-3 strong arXiv papers
2. Build GitHub reputation (1000+ stars)
3. Apply to postdoc positions with publication record
